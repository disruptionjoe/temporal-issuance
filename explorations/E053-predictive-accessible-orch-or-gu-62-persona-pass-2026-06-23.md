## Strongest Constructive Version

The strongest version is not "superposition is issuance." That collapses category levels.

The stronger target is:

> Predictive state is the observer/process-accessible representation of still-unresolved
> admissible extensions; accessible state is the quorum/record layer after an extension becomes
> usable for reconstruction. In standard quantum mechanics this is usually only a fixed-Hilbert-
> space update. It becomes Temporal Issuance-relevant only if the transition changes the
> admissible observable algebra, admissibility predicate, or source construction space in a way
> not pre-contained in a fixed `H`/`A_infty`/`Mu_infty`.

For Orch-OR:

> Microtubule quantum-optical states can be treated as a candidate physical predictive substrate.
> Anesthetic or cytoskeletal perturbations can be treated as probes of accessibility/finality
> cadence. The TI-relevant fixture is whether these perturbations reveal a growing observable
> algebra or source-side construction predicate, not merely decoherence or biochemical gating
> inside a fixed Hamiltonian.

For GU:

> GU signed-readout, observer-finality, TaF crosswalks, and geometric bundle/readout machinery
> can supply comparison targets and absorbers for the predictive-accessible split. They are
> context surfaces, not authority for TI claim promotion.

## Strongest Objection

Standard quantum superposition is a vector/state in a fixed Hilbert space or algebraic
framework. Measurement, decoherence, objective collapse, QBism, RQM, Quantum Darwinism, and
AB contextuality all already explain different versions of predictive/accessibility transition
without requiring D4 type-novel schema growth. Orch-OR, even if empirically strengthened, would
be an objective-collapse or quantum-biology mechanism unless it supplies a D4/H-growing witness.

## Phase 2: Heterodox Panel Synthesis

### Panel Finding 1: The Lens Is Useful, But Only As A Layer Split

The panel strongly accepts the predictive/accessibility vocabulary as a way to organize
observer-temporal questions. It rejects the direct identity:

```text
superposition = source-side issuance
```

The correct formal split is:

```text
fixed-H predictive uncertainty:
  value/eigenstate/update inside a pre-given observable algebra

H-growing / A-growing issuance:
  new observable type, admissibility predicate, or construction term becomes available
```

### Panel Finding 2: Orch-OR Is A Candidate Fixture, Not Evidence

The panel rates Orch-OR resonance as high conceptual fit and low evidential force. The
microtubule quantum-biology sources provide possible knobs:

- tryptophan-network superradiance / quantum yield / lifetime structure;
- microtubule or tubulin response under anesthetic or stabilizer perturbation;
- possible substrate-level differences between ordinary decoherence and altered accessibility.

But none of these establish:

```text
objective reduction in microtubules
conscious moments as OR events
TI-C020
H-growing observable algebra
source-side issuance
```

### Panel Finding 3: GU Cross-Link Should Be Context, Not Integration

`gu-formalization` can provide:

- signed-readout and observer-finality comparison surfaces;
- class-relative no-go discipline;
- bundle/connection/distortion-residue language;
- Higgs-illusion / fixed-source projection absorber analogies.

But this repo should not become a GU submodule and should not merge claim ledgers. The right
move is a cross-repo context note and a fixture specification.

### Panel Finding 4: The Strongest Fixture Is Fixed-H vs H-Growing

The next test should not ask "is superposition predictive?" The answer is interpretive and
absorbed.

The test should ask:

```text
Does the candidate physical substrate require a growing observable algebra / admissibility
predicate, or can every result be represented in a fixed Hilbert/operator/source space?
```

If fixed-H wins, the proposal is absorbed by standard QM/decoherence/objective-collapse
variants. If H-growing wins, it becomes a serious TI-C020 candidate.
