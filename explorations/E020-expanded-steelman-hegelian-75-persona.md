# E020: Expanded 75-Persona Steelman / Hegelian Review

## Purpose And Context

This is a W007-class steelman/Hegelian review of the Temporal Issuance research program after
the North Star reframing (RUN-0038) and the Phase 2B precision sequence (RUN-0039 through
RUN-0042). The review expands the standard 62-persona panel with 13 new personas selected for
their relevance to issuance, open-ended novelty, adaptive systems, and complexity.

The goal is not validation. The goal is maximum contact with reality: identifying what has
actually survived, what has merely been relocated, what remains vulnerable, what became
stronger, and which directions are highest leverage.

**Post-RUN-0042 state entering review:**
- North Star: "Why does reality remain capable of producing genuinely new structure?"
- Deepest claim: shared participation + ongoing issuance (TI-C019, speculative)
- D4: type-novel states not rule-generated from prior state
- OnlineSchemaSys: principled comparison class motivated by M2 (constructive types) and
  M3 (record formation); NAA structural theorem established
- Morphism-level non-embedding of OnlineSchemaSys into MetaCloSys: categorical fact

- TI-C020 (physical universe as OnlineSchemaSys): speculative, unearned
- Physics bridge: entirely unearned; BDO/ICO closed energy-momentum route

### Cluster A: Fundamental Physicists (12 personas)

*Representing: quantum gravity, quantum information, thermodynamics, cosmology, statistical
mechanics, QFT, GR specialists.*

**Q1 — Strongest surviving claim:** The layer separation is the real advance. Separating the
source-side (issuance) from the observer-side (temporal reconstruction) is a meaningful
structural claim that is not absorbed by any existing physics framework, because physics
frameworks are written from the external-description standpoint, not the process-capacity
standpoint. This is not nothing.

**Q2 — Hidden absorbers:** Spontaneous symmetry breaking (SSB) is the most dangerous absorber.
In the Standard Model, phase transitions in the early universe introduced new effective
particle types (the Higgs mechanism, quark-hadron transition, nucleosynthesis). If these are
D4 events, then D4 is absorbed by SSB dynamics in fixed Hilbert space. The discriminator
question: does SSB introduce genuinely new types or just new phases of existing fields? The
physicists are divided: some say SSB is clearly a D4 event (new effective degrees of freedom
not previously admissible), some say the Hilbert space is fixed and SSB is just state evolution.

**Q3 — Relocation vs. discovery:** The North Star shift from "why does time flow?" to "why is
reality open-ended?" is a genuine reframing, not just relocation. The original TI program was
running directly into thermodynamics and causal-set absorbers. The new framing avoids those
absorbers and opens a different research space. The shift is a real discovery of what the
project is actually about.

**Q4 — Issuance assessment:** Divided. D4 is meaningful if the physical state space genuinely
expands (Hilbert space growth, topology change, new effective field theories at different
energy scales). It is absorbed if the state space is fixed and D4 events are just exploring
pre-existing structure. The key unresolved physical question: is the early universe's
trajectory through its phase transitions better described as "exploring a pre-given phase
space" or "generating genuinely new types"? TI has not yet engaged this question precisely.

**Q5 — Open-endedness route:** Non-equilibrium thermodynamics and dissipative structures
(Prigogine) provide a physical route to open-endedness without requiring Hilbert space
expansion. Far-from-equilibrium systems self-organize into new structure that wasn't latent
in the initial conditions in any computable sense. This may be the physical analog of D4.

**Q6 — Physics relevance:** Plausible but distant. The Big Bang phase transitions, inflationary
new particle types, and the cosmological fine-tuning problem all suggest something like
issuance is in play. But "something like issuance" has not been formalized into physics
without importing ordinary physical structure. Verdict: plausible but distant.

**Q7 — Highest-leverage next step:** Connect D4 to the theory of phase transitions in the
early universe. Specifically: does the electroweak phase transition introduce new D4-type
entities (the W and Z bosons as genuinely new types not admissible before the transition),
or merely reveal latent structure? This is testable against the Standard Model and would
either earn or kill a physical connection.

**Key divergence within cluster:** The quantum information subgroup argues that quantum
measurement dynamics directly supports NAA: the no-cloning theorem and the causality of
measurement imply that future states are not available to present operations, which is exactly
NAA. This would make M1 (causal locality) derivable from quantum mechanics rather than
requiring prior causal structure. This is a significant finding that TI should pursue.

### Cluster D: Information Theorists and Computer Scientists (9 personas)

*Representing: Kolmogorov complexity, algorithmic information theory, computability,
complexity theory, information geometry.*

**Q1 — Strongest surviving claim:** The AC-6 (compression case / incomputability) analysis

**Q2 — Hidden absorbers:** Kolmogorov complexity is the most dangerous absorber. If new types

look like D4 events. But Kolmogorov complexity is defined relative to a fixed universal Turing
machine. Types with high K-complexity are still describable within the existing schema (they
have descriptions, just long ones). D4 requires types that are not instances of any existing
type — not just types with high complexity.

The distinction: a very complex string in {0,1}* is still a string. It's not a new type.
D4 requires a new type-label, not a new instance of string. So K-complexity absorbs D4 only
if "type" is conflated with "long description." If type-labels are primitive (not reducible
to descriptions), K-complexity does not absorb D4.

**Q3 — Relocation vs. discovery:** The constructive type theory connection (M2) is a discovery.
HoTT-style type formation is not the same as Kolmogorov complexity. They are different
frameworks answering different questions. TI connecting to HoTT is a genuine discovery of a
formal home, not relocation of existing ideas.

**Q4 — Issuance assessment:** D4 is meaningful, but the information theorist wants a measure.
How much issuance? When does a schema extension count as "new type" vs. "new instance"? The
lack of a quantitative measure is a weakness. Assembly Theory (Lee Cronin) provides one
candidate: the assembly index measures how many steps are needed to construct something. A D4
event would be one with infinite assembly index relative to the prior schema — it cannot be
assembled from prior type-elements at all. This is a precise formalization of D4(i).

**Q5 — Open-endedness route:** Computational irreducibility (Wolfram) provides one route:
processes that cannot be predicted without simulation. But irreducibility ≠ issuance (per
above). A better route is AIXI or universal induction theory: an agent with bounded resources
encountering a genuinely new type-class cannot compress their observations into existing
schemas. This connects D4 to bounded-resource learning theory.

**Q6 — Physics relevance:** Information geometry suggests a connection: when a new type
enters, the Fisher information metric of the system's possibility space changes qualitatively
(adds dimensions, not just moves within them). A metric dimension-adding event in information
geometry corresponds to D4. This is a concrete formalization that connects to statistical
physics.

**Q7 — Highest-leverage next step:** Define D4 using Assembly Theory. A D4 event is one where
the assembly index of the new state relative to the prior schema is undefined (unbounded) —
it cannot be assembled from prior type-elements. Test this against Cronin's framework.
