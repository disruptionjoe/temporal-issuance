## Extract

Mannheim's headline move: "dark matter is the gravitational pull of the rest of the universe." In
conformal (Weyl-squared, fourth-order) gravity, the potential around a galaxy is not determined by
local matter alone: the exterior solution picks up a linear term whose coefficient is sourced
cosmologically (homogeneous background) plus a quadratic term from exterior inhomogeneities
(reconstruction-grade recollection of Mannheim-Kazanas 1989 and the Mannheim-O'Brien 138-galaxy fits;
primary sources not checked for this note). Local dynamics is underdetermined from inside the region;
the deficit is filled by data from outside.
