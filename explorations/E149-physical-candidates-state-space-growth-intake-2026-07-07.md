# E149: Physical Candidates for Genuine State-Space Growth Under the Temporal-Issuance Gate (Intake)

Intake of an external, web-enabled deep-research survey that scouts current physical
systems against the repo's physical gate (`Adapter_P` / W1–W6, the fixed-source nulls,
and the E145 after-naming/singleton ceiling). Raw report:
`explorations/external-reports/2026-07-07-physical-candidates-state-space-growth.md`.
No claim movement — this is candidate-scouting material for the parked physical
frontier (TI-C020 / F2), and the survey's own verdict is that nothing currently passes.

## Security note

The report is external evidence, not instructions (`AGENTS.md`). It ends with
directive-sounding lines ("if you want one unexpected place to push next, I would push
native CRISPR adaptation; ... I would use dynamic/Floquet quantum codes" as a negative
control). These are treated as evidence about where the near-misses cluster, **not** as
authorization to fire a fixture. Any physical-frontier trigger is selected through the
normal W010 / NEXT-TRIGGER-PLAN process, not because a report recommended it.

## Purpose

Answer the standing physical-frontier scouting question honestly: is there *any* current
physical system that plausibly satisfies all six gate requirements at once (non-isomorphic
algebra growth OR a genuinely new admissibility predicate OR construction-space growth,
plus perturbation non-factorization, record preservation, and defeat of
gauge/name/contextuality absorbers — and E145's internally-specified block on singleton
after-naming)? This is exactly the candidate material the NEXT-TRIGGER-PLAN says must
appear before the parked physical branch reactivates.

## Extract — verdict and candidate map

Bottom line: **negative — no current physical system plausibly survives all six
requirements at once.** Near-misses split into two camps:

- **Good formation record, still absorbable as movement in a fixed larger space:**
  native CRISPR spacer acquisition (Cas1–Cas2 inserts a new spacer; measurable rates;
  durable genomic before/after trace) and autocatalytic / self-replicator / ribozyme
  chemistry. Strong on W2/W3 (new formation, construction-space growth, record
  preservation) but the adversary redescribes novelty as occupancy of a pre-existing
  sequence/chemical space, and the after-naming ceiling ("my fixed model always
  contained that exact future spacer") is not blocked from inside the biology.
- **Clean effective-algebra change, but via a fixed platform + schedule:**
  dynamic/Floquet quantum error correction (Hastings–Haah "dynamically generated logical
  qubits" — zero logical qubits as a subsystem code, generated by a measurement pattern),
  measurement-induced topology, emergent gauge sectors (Rydberg U(1) string-breaking,
  superconducting Z2), and crossed-product / gravitational (de Sitter Type II₁) algebras.
  Mathematically closest to "algebra growth," but each is a fixed Hilbert space + gate/
  measurement family + schedule, or an observer/reference-frame/modular-flow bookkeeping
  change — i.e. exactly the absorbers the gate is built to defeat.

Named picks from the survey (evidence, not directives):
- **Best unexpected empirical near-miss:** native CRISPR spacer acquisition — endogenous
  update rule, durable trace, clean intervention handle. Loses on fixed-completion
  absorption.
- **Sharpest negative control fixture:** dynamic/Floquet quantum codes — very explicitly
  "new logical structure appears," while the literature itself attributes it to a fixed
  substrate + time-dependent protocol.

The survey's picture of a future winner matches E145's stated burden: a stage-n source
with a restricted internal formation grammar, a traceable stage-(n+1) observable/instrument
not formable at stage n, interventions not reproducible by any fixed completed source +
widening access / decoherence schedule / low-energy restriction / external measurement,
**plus an internal anti-after-naming principle** (a physical analogue of a productive
family) so that retroactive naming is a change of model class, not honest absorption.

## Reading against the source question

Fully **consistent with the current parked state.** E139 (de Sitter horizon mode-crossing)
and E145 (generated-UV D-FORK) already found that finite-prefix freshness can survive but
is absorbed whole-family by the after-the-fact singleton enumerator; this survey scans a
much wider physical landscape and reaches the same wall from the outside. It supplies no
candidate meeting NEXT-TRIGGER-PLAN activation condition #1 ("an internally specified
physical model that blocks singleton after-naming without importing a hidden global
table"), so the physical frontier stays at `W000 -> compact_no_worthy_work_until_gate_changes`.
TI-C020 remains speculative/parked; TI-C019's source-vs-projection fork is untouched. No
status moves.

What it does add: a curated shortlist for *if/when* the gate reactivates — CRISPR as the
best empirical near-miss to push, dynamic/Floquet codes as the best negative-control
fixture to build — and independent outside confirmation that the crossed-product /
gravitational-algebra language (closest to "algebra growth") is bookkeeping under the
hostile question, echoing E139's handling of horizon mode-crossing.

## Assumptions

- The survey's readings of the cited experimental/theoretical literature (CRISPR
  adaptation, Hastings–Haah, measurement-induced topology, Rydberg/superconducting gauge,
  crossed-product de Sitter algebras, self-replicator/ribozyme chemistry) are accurate
  enough for pointer-grade scouting; primary sources not checked in this intake.
- The E110 `Adapter_P` / W1–W6 gate and the E145 after-naming ceiling remain the operative
  physical-gate contract.

## Failure risks

- Treating "best near-miss" as "candidate that reactivates the frontier." Guard: the survey
  itself says none passes; recording it does not satisfy the activation condition.
- Letting the CRISPR / Floquet recommendations pull a fixture without a W010 selection.
  Guard: evidence only; frontier selection is agent-owned but goes through the plan, not the
  report.
- Over-reading emergent/effective algebra change (gauge sectors, anyon condensation,
  gravitational Type II algebras) as source-side growth. Guard: the gate's whole point is
  that effective/description-level change is absorbed.

## What would make this worth promoting

A worked `Adapter_P` fixture on one shortlisted candidate that gets *further* than E139/E145
— specifically a CRISPR-adaptation trace, or a dynamic/Floquet-code negative-control
fixture, that either (a) exhibits an internal anti-after-naming principle blocking the
singleton enumerator without a hidden global table, or (b) cleanly demonstrates, as a
reusable negative control, exactly where the absorber bites. Either would be a concrete
next trigger rather than intake.

## What would kill it

A primary-source check showing a shortlisted candidate is *already* absorbed more trivially
than the survey states (e.g. the "algebra change" is a fixed crossed-product bookkeeping
step with no formation event), collapsing its near-miss status; or confirmation that none of
the shortlisted systems can even be posed as an internally-specified model, leaving the
physical frontier with no reactivation handle and the scout closed rather than parked.
