## Clarification (gap 2 residue): issuance is not passage

Issuance is not a claim that time objectively flows, that there is a moving
now, or that becoming is a physical process added on top of relativity. The
organizing question is why reality remains capable of producing genuinely new
structure. Temporal order, flow, and finality remain downstream observer-side
reconstructions from records. Any reading of Issue[S] as a passage or
global-present mechanism is a misreading; the E095 discipline (coherent
issuance under shared constraint, no universal clock, no preferred foliation)
governs.

## Gauntlet B: Everett / fixed-unitary-source absorber

Question:

```text
Under the repo's effect-typing, is Many-Worlds branching Issue[S] or
Project[O] — and either way, what follows for the hypothesis?
```

Expected polarity (recorded before the run, so the run can refute it): the
universal wavefunction plus unitary dynamics precontains every branch;

generator), arguably the purest physical instance of the fixed-source /

statement of the absorber the hypothesis must eventually beat.

Specification:

1. Type the Everett elements under Issue/Project/Finalize/Lose: unitary
   evolution (fixed generator), decoherence-selected branching (projection

   self-location uncertainty, or branch-relative indexical novelty, as
   Issue[S]. If a defensible typing exists, the hypothesis loses its
   distinctiveness claim against Everett and the result must be recorded as a
   major absorption.
3. Fixed-point contact: the E121 no_fixed_point_of_absorption theorem is the
   formal reason "extend the fixed source to include the escape" never closes

   generator, not a stage-extended family, so the theorem does NOT
   automatically apply. Whether the universal wavefunction counts as an
   internally-indexed family for any bounded observer is exactly the D-FORK
   restated in quantum dress; say so explicitly in the run.
4. Deliverable either way: a typed verdict file. "Everett = disclosure" makes
   the external-Platonist absorber concrete and physical; "Everett contains
   Issue[S]" is a kill-adjacent finding. Both are wins for the program's
