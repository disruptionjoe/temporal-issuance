# E191: R-Process Nucleosynthesis Candidate v0

## Question

Does r-process nucleosynthesis supply a physical source-side issuance witness?

The candidate is serious because neutron-rich ejecta can realize heavy nuclei,
actinides, and abundance peaks that were not present in the initial seed
material. If the source-side story were right, this would need to be more than
ordinary occupation of a predeclared nuclear possibility space.

## Frozen Fixed Rival

The rival is fixed before evaluation:

- nuclear species graph;
- nuclear mass table;
- neutron-capture cross sections;
- beta-decay rates;
- fission channels and recycling;
- seed abundance;
- neutron flux;
- entropy, electron fraction, and expansion trajectory;
- astrophysical ejecta context;
- stochastic ejecta and rate-uncertainty tape;
- measurement protocol and completed abundance history.

This rival is allowed to preserve abundance patterns, isotope ratios, decay
chains, kilonova light curves, spectral lines, temperature records, neutron
density records, and expansion records.

## Result

The candidate is killed as
`CANDIDATE_KILLED_FIXED_NUCLEAR_REACTION_NETWORK`.

The physical signal is real, but the source object is missing. Heavy-element
formation is represented as abundance flow through a fixed nuclear species
graph under fixed rates, fixed thermodynamic trajectory, fixed resources, and
fixed boundary/ejecta conditions. The successor context is an occupied region
of the same predeclared reaction network, not a source-owned expansion of the
nuclear admissibility law.

CompletionClass v1 returns `PHYSICAL_PREDICTIVE_ABSORPTION`.

## Exact Missing Object

The resurrection burden is a source-owned nuclear species generation law: a
native physical source law in which nucleosynthesis creates a new nuclear
species graph, admissibility predicate, reaction grammar, or construction
space not representable as fixed nuclear mass and rate tables, fixed seed
abundance, fixed neutron flux, fixed electron fraction, fixed expansion
trajectory, fixed ejecta context, fission recycling, stochastic variation,
observer access, or completed history.

It would also need an internal anti-after-naming principle plus W4 perturbation
non-factorization under the same preserved abundance, isotope-ratio,
decay-chain, spectral, light-curve, temperature, neutron-density, and expansion
records.
