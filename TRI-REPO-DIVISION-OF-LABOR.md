## The joint effort (stated with guards intact)

The three repositories independently drove three different questions to the
same structural residue:

> A bounded system, complete and self-consistent in itself, provably cannot
> supply one specific thing from inside — and the missing thing lives at its
> boundary. The honest ledger for that boundary is what the region can do,
> not what it can see.

- **temporal-issuance:** the `Issue[S]^LC` gate's no-hidden-future-oracle
  condition is precisely "the interior cannot precontain its own extension";
  if genuine novelty enters, it enters across the interface.
- **time-as-finality:** record/order facts are capability facts, not
  statistical facts; the arrow is capability loss across an access boundary.
- **gu-formalization:** the interior matter geometry is vectorlike and cannot
  force its own chirality / generation count; the count enters as external
  boundary data.

**The identity claim ("one object, three shadows") is explicitly NOT made.**
Per `explorations/E119-tri-repo-capability-burden-retyping-2026-07-02.md`, the
capability framing is adopted as a sharpened adversarial burden, not as
evidence. No identity claim before at least two adapter contracts prove out.
Current status: same shape, three independent arrivals, identity untested.

## Verification tiers (shared cross-repo vocabulary, ratified by Joe 2026-07-02)

Each repo's native grade vocabulary remains authoritative for its own claims
(here: the claim-ledger statuses and the theorem-of-the-fixture grades).
This three-tier overlay is for cross-repo communication and public
writeups, so "internally verified" never masquerades as "established":

1. **recorded** — result exists with runnable artifacts; not yet survived
   adversarial review.
2. **internally established** — survived the repo's own hostile review, with
   numbers independently re-derived from scratch and everything reproducible
   from the tree. (The E120/E121 kernel-checked Lean suite is the strong
   sub-case: the proofs are machine-verified by independent software; the
   modeling faithfulness remains internal.) Maps to the standard
   reproduced-vs-replicated distinction: reproduced, not yet replicated.
3. **externally established** — verified outside the process: independent
   replication, peer review, or named-specialist sign-off.

Single-process ceiling: because internal reviewers are spawned by the same
process that produced the result, no internal step — however adversarial —
can promote a result to tier 3.
