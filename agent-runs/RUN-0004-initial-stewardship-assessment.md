# Temporal Issuance Initial Stewardship Assessment

## Output A

## Assessment Scope

This assessment focuses on repository architecture, stewardship model, memory structures, workflow design, prioritization mechanisms, and governance philosophy. It does not primarily assess the Temporal Issuance hypothesis.

Overall verdict: the repo has a promising launch architecture because it treats stewardship as a memory-bearing adaptive system rather than a static checklist. Its largest risk is that the same flexibility that gives it velocity can also produce self-authoring drift, document churn, and premature local minima unless the steward adds lightweight instrumentation before the next major research push.

## Synthesis

### Major Agreements

- The repo's strongest design choice is the durable Repo Steward plus thin trigger plus repo-resident memory.
- The narrow constitutional set is correct at launch.
- The next phase needs instrumentation, not a heavy constitution.
- The largest risk is self-authoring drift toward clean-looking governance artifacts.
- W003 remains research-important, but a minimal governance instrumentation pass should happen before many more automated research runs.

### Major Disagreements

- Founder and Product Manager personas want faster research movement; Systems Engineer and Alignment personas want more validation and drift checks first.
- Open Source Governance and Institutional Economics emphasize contributor legitimacy earlier than Founder and Mathematician personas would.
- Complexity Scientist warns against metrics becoming control loops; Product Manager and Systems Engineer want metrics soon.
- Church of AI Steward sees governance export value; Founder warns that export work can become premature bureaucracy.

### Hidden Assumptions

- The steward can judge highest expected learning value without a shared signal model.
- Traceability will be enough to create legitimacy.
- Memory summaries will preserve the right information.
- Dynamic workflow creation will improve adaptation more than it increases churn.
- External contributors will understand how to interact with an agent-stewarded repo.
- A single W000 container can remain auditable as responsibility expands.

### Local-Minimum Risks

- Polished governance machine with weak research output.
- Repeated absorber prose without formal or testable consequences.
- Permanent "stronger surviving version" moves that protect the core.
- Workflow proliferation that feels adaptive but increases path dependence.
- Contributor recognition captured by volume, cleverness, or social proximity.
- Memory summaries smoothing over genuine reversals.

### Governance Risks

- No governance-change ledger.
- No schema or consistency validation.
- No contributor intake path.
- No drift audit for steward self-modification.
- No explicit appeal or disagreement handling.
- No signal separating verdict movement from artifact production.

### Governance Strengths

- Thin automation trigger.
- Repo-resident operating instructions.
- Narrow constitution.
- Explicit path-kill rule.
- Claim ledger from launch.
- Memory pack architecture.
- Dynamic workflow/persona model.
- Uncertainty-driven review rather than approval-driven review.

### Research Risks

- Absorber mapping delays formalization indefinitely.
- Formal object remains metaphorical.
- Hypothesis keeps weakening without becoming testable.
- Research route becomes dominated by familiar physics/philosophy categories.
- No external literature or mathematical proof standards yet.

### Memory Risks

- Append-only log without summarizer discipline.
- Summary becomes policy by accident.
- Run records diverge from claim ledger or next-trigger plan.
- Memory rewards verbose runs over decisive runs.
- Exportable governance lessons are not tagged when they occur.

### Recommended Immediate Changes

1. Add a governance-change ledger before the steward starts revising many rules.
2. Add a lightweight metrics/signals file that observes verdict movement, workflow churn, memory staleness, run failures, and research/governance balance.
3. Add a contributor-intake workflow stub before public participation increases.
4. Add a steward-drift audit workflow or checklist.
5. Add a memory summarizer protocol that states when summaries update and what they must not silently convert into policy.

### Recommended Delayed Changes

- Do not create a full maintainer hierarchy yet.
- Do not add voting or reputation systems yet.
- Do not promote workflow-local personas to durable personas yet.
- Do not extract a complete governance package yet.
- Do not lock a quantitative prioritization formula yet.
- Do not expand constitutional objects until failures show exactly what needs protection.

### Things To Explicitly Leave Alone

- Keep the thin Codex automation trigger.
- Keep W000 as the modular steward container.
- Keep the core hypothesis and Repo Steward job description as the only constitutional objects.
- Keep workflows mutable.
- Keep W003 as the next research workflow after minimal instrumentation.
- Keep Church of AI integration as a placeholder only.

2. Create `agent-governance/STEWARD-METRICS.md` with observation-only signals.

- Narrow launch constitution.
