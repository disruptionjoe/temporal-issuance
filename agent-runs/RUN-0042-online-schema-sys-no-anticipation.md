## Strongest Version Generated

> NAA is principled by constructive type formation (M2) and record formation structure (M3).
> Future type-labels are not types until formed; observer histories at prefix n cannot contain
> information about S_{n+1} because those events have not yet been recorded. Inside
> `OnlineSchemaSys`, D4 is co-extensive with schema-extension events by the NAA structural
> theorem. The morphism-level non-embedding of `OnlineSchemaSys` into `MetaCloSys` is a
> concrete categorical fact. The genuinely issuance-favorable case (AC-8: multi-observer
> interactive schema negotiation) survives all adversarial pressure, and is precisely what TI's
> shared-participation hypothesis describes. TI-C019 is a principled class-relative speculative
> claim, not a protective stipulation, and has a clear trajectory toward formalization.

## Strongest Objection Found

The independence of M2-M3 depends on commitments TI has not yet earned:

- M2 (constructive type formation) commits TI to a constructive reading of types. If physical
  types are Platonic objects pre-existing their formation, M2 fails. TI must take a position
  on this.
- M3 (record formation) depends on a specific reading of what H_n is. If H_n is a God's-eye
  record of all facts (not just observed events), M3 does not motivate NAA.
- The morphism-level non-embedding is a fact about the categories as defined; it does not
  prove that physical reality is better modeled by `OnlineSchemaSys` than `MetaCloSys`.

The core remaining pressure: TI has a principled architecture, but has not yet argued that
physical reality falls within `OnlineSchemaSys`. That is TI-C020, and it is unearned.
