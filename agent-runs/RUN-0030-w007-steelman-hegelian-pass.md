### Most Important Hegelian Syntheses

**Synthesis A: Formal Structure vs. Physical Interpretation.**
The central tension in the repo: the formal structure (`ExtCat`, morphism-level invariants, extension
categories) is solid, but the physical interpretation (energy-momentum bridge) is path-killed. The
synthesis: separate the formal mathematics from the physical interpretation. Publish the formal results

**Synthesis B: Category vs. Control Case.**
BDO and ICO are theorems about a specific control case (Poincare-invariant Lorentzian field theory on
fixed `(M, eta)`). The extension category is a general mathematical object. The synthesis: the theorems
are correct, but their scope is narrower than they may appear. They close one control case, not all
possible physics settings.

**Synthesis C: Source-Side vs. Observer-Side.**
The repo has focused on whether `Ext_S` can reach observer-accessible physics (energy-momentum).
But many personas (55-62, 9, 35) show that the source-side structure is interesting in its own right.
The synthesis: Temporal Issuance may be more important as a theory of source-side record structure
than as a theory of observer-accessible physics.

**Synthesis D: Physical Bridge vs. Mathematical Framework.**
The most productive reframing: Temporal Issuance is not a claim that competes with general relativity
or quantum field theory. It is a formal framework (extension categories) within which physical theories
can be formulated. The question is not "can `Ext_S` derive energy-momentum?" but "which physical
theories have the property that their boundary-value structure is formalizeable as an extension category?"
