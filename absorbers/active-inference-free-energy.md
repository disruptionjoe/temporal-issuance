# Absorber: Active Inference / Variational Free Energy

Pointer-grade intake (2026-07-07). Primary sources NOT yet checked. This is the strongest known
formal framework that could ABSORB the issuance-as-novelty hypothesis; it is recorded here as a
kill test, not as support. Per TI method, a clean absorption is a success.

## The absorber claim (to attack)

Every phenomenon TI attributes to *issuance* (the admission of genuinely new possibility) is already
owned by active inference as *disclosure under a fixed generative model*: an agent with a Markov
blanket minimizes variational free energy (prediction error / surprise) by approximate Bayesian
inference; "exploration," "novelty," and "new capability" are epistemic-value terms in the SAME
expected-free-energy functional, i.e. the agent reducing uncertainty about a model it already carries.
On this reading, nothing new crosses the interface; the agent discloses what its generative model
already implied. This lands directly on TI's D-FORK (issuance vs disclosure) and on the
`FORMAL-DEFINITION-REPAIR` burden ("does any source-extension rule change observer capabilities in a
way readout cannot already express?").

## Why it is a serious threat

- Active inference is a complete, quantitative variational framework with an action principle
  (free-energy minimization) — exactly the kind of "checkable filter" the rigor bar demands.
- It natively carries the boundary/interface (Markov blanket) and the observer (generative model),
  so the two-reference capability structure (region-level vs individual-agent) maps onto it without
  strain — which is precisely why manufactured convergence is a risk here.
- Levin's competency extension already scales it across levels (MCA), so the "synthesized through the
  system" governance intuition also has an active-inference reading (nested Markov blankets), meaning
  the governance-of-issuance idea does not escape the absorber for free.

## What TI must show to survive the absorber (the named surplus)

The issuance hypothesis survives ONLY if it exhibits a capability change that is NOT expressible as
free-energy minimization against any fixed generative model — i.e. a change to the model's own
support / hypothesis space that is not itself a higher-level inference step. Candidate surplus:
- A capability gain with zero statistical trace on the region (T395 / TaF), where active inference
  predicts a trace (expected free energy is a functional of the agent's own predicted observations).
- Genuine hypothesis-space extension (new admissible possibility) vs. Bayesian model *selection*
  within a fixed hypothesis space — the classic "where do new hypotheses come from" gap that Bayesian
  inference is known not to close from inside (flag: from-memory; verify against the active-inference
  literature on structure learning / model expansion).

## Kill / absorb conditions

- **Absorbed (TI success):** if every proposed issuance instance is re-expressible as expected-free-
  energy reduction over a fixed model, issuance collapses to disclosure and the source residue is
  archived. This would be a clean, publishable negative.
- **Survives:** if a capability/hypothesis-space extension is exhibited that provably is not a
  free-energy-minimizing inference over any fixed model (the T395 zero-trace route is the sharpest
  candidate), the absorber bounds but does not eat the hypothesis.
