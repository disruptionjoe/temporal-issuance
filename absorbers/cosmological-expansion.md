# Cosmological Expansion

Threat:

Temporal issuance may confuse global expansion, horizon structure, or cosmological boundary conditions with a deeper issuance process.

Key test:

Can issuance be defined in non-expanding models or models with different cosmological boundary conditions?

## RUN-0010 Initial Mapping

Cosmological expansion is a threat to any Temporal Issuance language that treats "growth" as automatically fundamental.

What it explains:

- large-scale change in distances, horizons, and cosmic history
- boundary-condition pressure on arrow-like narratives
- the temptation to read global growth as ontological becoming

What it does not yet explain:

- local observer record cadence
- access-limited reconciliation between observers
- whether constraint fixedness has a formal role independent of scale-factor dynamics

Category risk:

Cosmological expansion is a physical model feature. Temporal Issuance is currently framed as an ontological or formal substrate claim. Comparing them directly can create a layer error.

Current verdict:

Cosmological expansion is not the leading absorber for the whole hypothesis, but it is a serious absorber for any `mu` or "growth" language that behaves like volume, scale, horizon, or boundary-condition structure.

W002 implication:

Test `mu` against expansion, volume, entropy, and information before treating issuance measure as distinct.
